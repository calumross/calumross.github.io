Image Research

IT'S A ME, WARIO

Scottish Water

I love this Scottish Water animation, it has a nice rounded, friendly look, and the 3D animation in a 2D style works well. It has a storyline that gives it some character, it's snappy and to the point. Maybe doing multiple shorter videos would be a smarter approach, since these videos are focused on a social media audience.

Bolton Council

This animation for Bolton Council provides all the necessary information for each bin, but the narration is soul destroying, and there's not a lot of fun in the animation. Informative, but not attention keeping.

Aberdeenshire Council

Done by Stuart on the course 2 years ago, this animation flows really well, has a more engaging narration, and there's a lot of fun things happening around the screen at all times, creating a more interesting video. Keeping that same business going on in my own animation is going to be a little harder in stop motion but that's what I want to show I can do.

Coca Cola

Built upon a similar premise to my project - kind of showing the process of Coca Cola's bottle recycling - through a trope, a love story. I was particularly interested in how they reformed the bottles to represent other objects, I plan on melting PET bottles and forming them into the wildlife growing at the end of the animation.

Coca Cola Making Of

The making of process for the Coca Cola animation, really useful for seeing techniques that could be used, especially for layering animation over each other and the look of the plant bottles I might use.

Honda Paper

This animation's really incredible, all of Honda's videos are a constant source of inspiration. It took months to make though, somewhat out of my range! Using real objects though and the smooth flow of this animation is what I'm aiming for.

Walk Deformation Test

My own video research into how the character may deform behind a bottle if it was etched and shown from the other side. This video was also used for me to get a more natural movement withing the character.

Bottle Etch Test

My first test using the Armour Etch on bottles to see how well it works, how visible it appears on the brown bottles, and whether this could be a viable way to animate roughly a minute of video. On reflection I feel the etching worked really well, and at 8 frames per second it has a nice stop motion feel and also could be possible for me. the brown bottles are completely opaque, so I could use one bottle for 2 or 3 frames, and any time I want the character to go behind I could use clear, maybe green bottles?

PES: Submarine Sandwich

PES's animations are always incredible, the set design is extensive, and the time taken to animate himself in stop motion shows such a huge level of skill. The wit in the choice of object replacement makes each animation beautiful and awe inspiring.

Secret World of Stuff

Looking at ways of bringing character into everyday objects, maybe as a quicker method of having the character in the world without having to physically create each character frame on an object.

Guldies: Magic Paper

Great stop motion, and this animation in particular really has a beautiful style of water animation, in this case paper. I'll also experiment with Plasticine as liquid.

Guldies: Hamburger

Another great Guldies animation, this one is interesting to look at as a way of depicting a process, the transition between materials is something to experiment with for my own animation.

Joe and Becky

Joe and Becky's animations are a mixup of film and animation, and their set and props design is amazing. In the interest of time, maybe a combination of these techniques would benefit my animation?
